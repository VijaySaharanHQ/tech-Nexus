# 🚀 Digital Innovation Lab

Welcome to the **Digital Innovation Lab** at Tech Nexus!

This is the Research & Development (R&D) wing of our ecosystem. It is a playground for creators, hardware hackers, game developers, and aspiring founders to turn theoretical knowledge into real-world prototypes.

## 🗂️ Lab Directory

Choose your domain and start building:

### 1. [IoT & Automation](./iot-automation/)
Bridge the gap between software and the physical world.
* **Topics:** Arduino sketches, Raspberry Pi setups, home automation scripts (Home Assistant), and sensor integration.
* **Resources:** Wiring diagrams, hardware requirements lists, and micro-controller code.

### 2. [Game Development](./game-dev/)
Design, program, and publish interactive experiences.
* **Topics:** Unity (C#), Godot (GDScript), Unreal Engine (C++), and web-based games.
* **Resources:** Game mechanics scripts, open-source 2D/3D assets, and physics logic examples.

### 3. [Startups & MVPs](./startups/)
From napkin sketch to Minimum Viable Product (MVP).
* **Topics:** Product Requirements Documents (PRDs), system architecture designs, and rapid prototyping templates.
* **Resources:** Pitch deck outlines, database schemas, and business logic boilerplate.

---

## 🤝 How to Contribute to the Innovation Lab

Building complex projects requires excellent documentation. Please follow these rules:

1. **Hardware Projects:** If submitting to the IoT folder, you *must* include a clear list of required physical components and a wiring diagram (images are highly encouraged).
2. **Game Assets:** Ensure any 3D models, audio files, or textures submitted are truly open-source and do not violate third-party copyright. Keep file sizes optimized.
3. **Startup MVPs:** Include a `README.md` in your specific project folder detailing what problem the MVP solves and how to run it locally.

Please read our master [Contributing Guidelines](../../CONTRIBUTING.md) before submitting a Pull Request!
