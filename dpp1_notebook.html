DPP-01: Electrostatic Potential and Capacitance

Q1. An electron enters between two horizontal plates separated by 2mm and having a potential difference of 1000V. The force on the electron is?
Solution: Force F = qE = q(V/d) = (1.6x10^-19 * 1000) / (2x10^-3) = 8.0x10^-14 N
Q2. Two small spheres each carrying a charge q are placed r metre apart. If one of the spheres is taken around the other one in a circular path of radius r, the work done will be equal to?
Solution: The work done is zero because moving a charge along an equipotential path requires no work.
Q3. Two equal charges q are placed at a distance of 2a and a third charge -2q is placed at the midpoint. The potential energy of the system is?
Solution: U = k[q^2/2a + q(-2q)/a + q(-2q)/a] = -7q^2 / (8*pi*epsilon_0*a)
Q4. An alpha particle is accelerated through a potential difference of 10^6 volt. Its kinetic energy will be?
Solution: KE = qV = (2e) * 10^6 = 2 MeV
Q5. A charge of 5C is given a displacement of 0.5m. The work done in the process is 10J. The potential difference between the two points will be?
Solution: V = W/q = 10J / 5C = 2V
Q6. Two positive point charges of 12uC and 8uC are 10cm apart. The work done in bringing them 4cm closer is?
Solution: W = k*q1*q2*(1/r_final - 1/r_initial) = 5.8 J
Q7. The work done in bringing a 20 coulomb charge from point A to point B for distance 0.2m is 2J. The potential difference between the two points will be?
Solution: V = W/q = 2J / 20C = 0.1 V
Q8. Three charges +Q, +q, +q are placed at the vertex of an equilateral triangle. For what value of Q, the electrostatic potential energy of the system is zero?
Solution: U = k/r * (2Qq + q^2) = 0 -> Q = -q/2
Q9. Identify the wrong statement regarding the electrical potential energy of a proton-electron system.
Solution: Wrong: Electrical potential energy increases when separation decreases; it actually becomes more negative (decreases).
Q10. A capacitor of 4uF is connected to a 200V supply. The charge on the capacitor is?
Solution: Q = CV = (4x10^-6 F) * (200 V) = 8x10^-4 C = 0.8 mC
